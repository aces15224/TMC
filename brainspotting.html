What is Brainspotting?

Brainspotting is a therapeutic technique developed by Dr. David Grand in 2003 to help individuals process and release emotional trauma, stress, and psychological pain. It combines elements of trauma therapy, EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing), and body-centered therapies, based on the idea that where you look can influence how you feel. Using specific eye positions, known as "brainspots," the therapist targets emotional experiences or trauma stored in the brain.

Once a brainspot is located, the client focuses on the emotional or physical sensations tied to the issue, enabling deep, unconscious processing of trauma. Unlike traditional talk therapy, which relies on verbal expression, Brainspotting facilitates healing at a non-conscious level, allowing clients to release unresolved emotions more efficiently and effectively.
